ABOUT US👩‍❤️‍👨

My love,

I want to talk about us. Not where we're going, not where we've been — just us, as we are, as we exist right now in this strange and beautiful thing we've built together. Because I don't think I stop enough to look at what we have and simply appreciate it. I don't think I say it enough — how remarkable it is, what we are to each other, what this feels like from the inside when I'm paying close attention.

Us is something I never quite knew how to picture before you. I had ideas, of course — vague notions of what a relationship should look and feel like, shaped by things I'd seen and stories I'd heard. But none of those ideas looked like this. None of them felt like this. What we have doesn't fit neatly into any template I had prepared, and I mean that as the highest compliment I know how to give. We are something I didn't know to hope for until it was already happening.

I think about the way we work together — how we have somehow, without ever sitting down to negotiate it, figured out each other's rhythms. The way I know when you need space and when you need to be held. The way you know when I'm not okay even before I do. The quiet language we've developed between us, made entirely of small gestures and familiar silences and looks that say more than a paragraph ever could. That doesn't happen by accident. That is something two people build, carefully and consistently, over time. And we built it. Together.

I think about the way we laugh. There is a specific kind of laughter that only exists between us — the kind that starts over something ridiculous and ends with both of us breathless, unable to even remember what started it. The kind that makes me think, in the middle of it, that this is it. This is what I want. Not just the grand romantic gestures, not just the milestone moments — but this. This exact laughter, with this exact person, in whatever ordinary setting we happen to be in. That laughter is one of my favorite things about us and I don't say it nearly enough.

I think about how we handle the hard parts too, because us is not just the easy and beautiful moments. Us is also the times we didn't quite understand each other, the moments when we said the wrong thing or went quiet when we should have spoken up. Us is the tension and the making up, the working through and the coming back to each other. And what I've realized is that the hard parts don't diminish what we have — they define it. Every time we've chosen to work through something instead of walk away from it, we have added another layer to what we are. We are deeper because of those moments, not in spite of them.

There is a comfort in us that I want to name, because I think it deserves to be named. The kind of comfort where I don't have to perform, don't have to be impressive, don't have to hold my life together in a certain way just to be worthy of your company. Around you, I am just myself — and somehow, that has always been enough. Do you know how rare that is? To find someone in whose presence you can completely exhale? That is what you are to me. You are someone I exhale around. And I hope, more than anything, that I am that for you too.

I think about how much we have already been through — the seasons we've navigated, the moments that tested us, the days that were harder than either of us expected. And I think about how, at the end of every one of those moments, we were still here. Still us. Still choosing each other without making a big production of it, just quietly, the way people do when something matters enough to keep. That continuity means something to me. It means more than I know how to properly articulate.

I also think about how much we have yet to discover about each other. Because as well as I know you, I know there are still layers I haven't reached, still versions of you I haven't met, still things you'll say someday that will catch me completely off guard and make me fall a little more. That excites me. The idea that knowing you is not a destination but an ongoing thing — that there will always be more of you to understand, more of us to uncover — that is something I find genuinely thrilling. I am not tired of learning you. I don't think I ever will be.

Us, at its core, is two people who found each other and decided — without announcing it, without making it dramatic — that this was worth it. That the other person was worth it. Worth the effort, the vulnerability, the risk of loving someone fully and hoping they love you back just as completely. We made that decision. We keep making it. And every time we do, quietly and without fanfare, we are choosing something I think is genuinely rare and genuinely precious.

So I just want to say — I am proud of us. I am grateful for us. I do not take us for granted, not for a single day. What we have is not something that comes along easily, and I know that. I hold it carefully, the way you hold something you're afraid to break, the way you hold something that has become completely irreplaceable.

Us is my favorite thing. It always will be. 🤍.
