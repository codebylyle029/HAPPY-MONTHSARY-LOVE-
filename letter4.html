OUR DREAMS! 💭

My love,

I want to tell you about a dream I have. Not the kind that visits you while you sleep, but the kind that lives quietly in the back of your mind during the day — the kind you return to when life gets heavy and you need something warm to hold onto. I have been holding onto this one for a while now, and it has your name written all over it.

I dream about a morning that hasn't happened yet. A morning where I wake up and the first thing I see is you — not as my girlfriend, but as my wife. Where the ring on your finger has been there long enough to feel like it belongs, and the life we share has settled into something comfortable and full and entirely ours. I don't know exactly when that morning comes, but I know it's the one I'm working toward. I know it's the one I want.

I dream about a home we build together. Not just four walls and a roof, but a place that feels like both of us — where your taste and mine somehow meet in every corner, where the kitchen smells like something we're cooking together on a slow weekend afternoon, where the couch has that one spot that belongs to no one else but us. A home where we have our routines, our rhythms, the small rituals that nobody else would understand but that mean everything to the two of us. I want that home with you. I want to unlock that door every evening knowing you're on the other side of it.

I dream about the proposal, too. About the moment I finally get to ask you the question I've already answered in my heart. I think about how nervous I'll be, how my hands might shake a little even though I've never been more sure of anything in my life. I think about your face in that moment — the exact expression you'll make, the way your eyes will look — and even just imagining it makes something in my chest feel impossibly full. I want to give you that moment. I want to give you a story worth telling.

I dream about our wedding day. About seeing you walk toward me and feeling like the entire world has narrowed down to just you, just that aisle, just that moment. I think about holding your hands and meaning every single word of whatever I promise you, knowing that these are not just words for the occasion but a commitment I have been building up to for a long time. I want to marry you. Not someday in a distant, vague way — but with intention, with excitement, with the full weight of knowing exactly what I'm doing and being grateful for every second of it.

I dream about what comes after the wedding too — the part that doesn't get romanticized as much but that I think about just as deeply. The ordinary Tuesday evenings. The grocery runs. The bills we'll figure out together, the decisions we'll make as a team, the seasons of life that will ask things of us we're not fully prepared for yet. I dream about navigating all of that with you — not because it sounds glamorous, but because doing hard, real, unglamorous things with you sounds like exactly the kind of life I want.

I dream about us growing. About the version of you I haven't met yet — the one that exists ten, fifteen, twenty years from now, shaped by everything life will have brought us through by then. I think about how I'll still be learning you, still discovering new things to love about you, still choosing you the same way I do now but with so much more history behind it. I want to know every version of you that comes. I want to grow old in the direction of you.

I dream about the family we might build. About a home that gets a little louder, a little fuller, a little more chaotic in the best possible way. About watching you become a mother someday and feeling something I don't even have the right words for yet — something deep and proud and overwhelming in the most beautiful sense. About the two of us figuring out parenthood together, imperfectly and lovingly, the way we figure everything else out. I dream about that life with you, and it feels like the most natural thing in the world.

I dream about getting old with you. About a version of us that has been through so much together that we don't even need to finish each other's sentences — we just know. Where we can sit in comfortable silence and feel completely at home in it. Where the love between us isn't loud anymore because it doesn't need to be — it's just there, woven into everything, as steady and certain as breathing. I want to reach that version of us. I want to earn it, year by year, with you.

And through every part of that dream — the proposal, the wedding, the home, the hard seasons, the quiet evenings, the growing old — there is one thing that never changes, one constant that holds all of it together. It is you. Always you. You are not just in the dream, my love. You are the reason the dream exists in the first place.

So when you wonder if I see a future with you, if you are someone I imagine standing beside me in the biggest and smallest moments of my life — I hope this answers it. Completely. Without a single doubt left standing.

I am dreaming of a life with you. And I cannot wait for the day those dreams become our reality. 🤍.
